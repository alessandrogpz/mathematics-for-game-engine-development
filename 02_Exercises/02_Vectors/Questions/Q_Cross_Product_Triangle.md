# Question
## Part 1: Calculation
A triangle is defined in 3D space by the points $A(1, 0, 1)$, $B(3, 2, 1)$, and $C(1, 2, 3)$.
1. Calculate vectors $\vec{AB}$ and $\vec{AC}$.
2. Compute their cross product $\vec{AB} \times \vec{AC}$.
3. Use the result to find the area of the triangle.

## Part 2: Conceptual Understanding
1. If you calculated $\vec{AC} \times \vec{AB}$ instead, how would the resulting vector change? What property of the cross product does this demonstrate?
2. How is the area of the triangle related to the magnitude of the cross product? Why is there a factor of $1/2$?
3. Does the cross product exist for 2D vectors? If so, how do we typically represent the result?
