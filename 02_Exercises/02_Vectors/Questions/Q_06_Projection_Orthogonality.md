# Question
## Part 1: Calculation
Given vectors $\vec{a} = (3, -1, 2)$ and $\vec{b} = (1, 2, 2)$:
1. Calculate the scalar projection of $\vec{a}$ onto $\vec{b}$ ($\text{comp}_{\vec{b}}\vec{a}$).
2. Calculate the vector projection of $\vec{a}$ onto $\vec{b}$ ($\text{proj}_{\vec{b}}\vec{a}$).
3. Find a vector $\vec{c}$ such that $\vec{c} = \vec{a} - \text{proj}_{\vec{b}}\vec{a}$.

## Part 2: Conceptual Understanding
1. Show mathematically that the vector $\vec{c}$ you found is orthogonal to $\vec{b}$. 
2. What is the geometric interpretation of $\vec{c}$ in relation to $\vec{a}$ and $\vec{b}$?
3. If the dot product $\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b}$ were negative, what would that tell you about the direction of the "shadow" (projection) relative to $\vec{b}$?
