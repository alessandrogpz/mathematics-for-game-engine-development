# Question
## Part 1: Calculation
Given vectors $\vec{a} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ -1 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}$ and $\vec{b} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}$:
1. Calculate the dot product $\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b}$.
2. Calculate the magnitude of both vectors.
3. Find the angle $\theta$ between them.

## Part 2: Conceptual Understanding
1. Without calculating, what does the sign of $\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b}$ tell you about the angle $\theta$ (is it acute, obtuse, or right)?
2. If we replaced $\vec{b}$ with a vector $\vec{c}$ such that $\vec{a} \cdot \vec{c} = 0$, what is the geometric relationship between $\vec{a}$ and $\vec{c}$?
3. How does the dot product relate to the concept of "projection" that we studied?
