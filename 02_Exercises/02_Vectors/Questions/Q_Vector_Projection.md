# Question
## Part 1: Calculation
You have a velocity vector $\vec{v} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ -3 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}$ and a surface normal vector $\vec{n} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}$.
1. Calculate the scalar projection of $\vec{v}$ onto $\vec{n}$.
2. Calculate the vector projection of $\vec{v}$ onto $\vec{n}$.

## Part 2: Conceptual Understanding
1. In this specific scenario, what does the vector projection represent physically? (Hint: The normal vector points "out" from a surface).
2. If the normal vector $\vec{n}$ was not a unit vector, how would the formula for $\text{proj}_{\vec{n}}\vec{v}$ change?
3. How can you use the vector projection to find the component of $\vec{v}$ that is parallel to the surface (perpendicular to $\vec{n}$)?
