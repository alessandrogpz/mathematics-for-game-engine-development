# Question
## Part 1: Calculation
Given matrix $M = \begin{bmatrix} 4 & 7 \\ 2 & 6 \end{bmatrix}$:
1. Calculate the determinant of $M$.
2. Construct the inverse matrix $M^{-1}$ using the adjugate method.
3. Verify your answer by calculating the product $M M^{-1}$.

## Part 2: Conceptual Understanding
1. Based on your calculation of $\det(M)$, is $M$ a singular or non-singular matrix?
2. If you were to multiply every element of $M$ by $2$, how would the new determinant relate to the original one?
3. Prove that for any $2 \times 2$ matrix  $M$, the inverse matrix $M^{-1}$ exists if and only if $ad - bc \neq 0$.
